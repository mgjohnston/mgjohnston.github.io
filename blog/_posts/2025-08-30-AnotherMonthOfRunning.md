Five and a half years after the [first month of 5k a day](/OneMonthOfRunning/), I went again - this time from mid-June through to the end of August. Two and a half months of (nearly) daily 5k+, with the odd 10k thrown in to break it up.

The big difference between 2020-me and 2025-me: pace. Day 1 in 2020 was a 35-minute 5k. The streak threw up two parkrun PBs:

- **9 Aug — Bedgebury Pinetum, 24:35** — my first ever sub-25 parkrun (and 25th parkrun overall)
- **23 Aug — Portobello, Edinburgh, 24:05** — knocked another 30 seconds off two weeks later on parkrun tourism

| Month | 5k+ runs | Total km | Best parkrun |
|---|---|---|---|
| Jun (from 18th) | 6 | 33 | 27:22 (Ashbourne) |
| Jul | 27 | 152 | 25:21 (Squerryes Winery) |
| Aug | 25 | 138 | **24:05 (Portobello)** |
| **Total** | **58** | **~323** | **24:05** |

The daily 5k splits sat mostly between 25 and 30 minutes on the back-to-back days; the PBs came on parkruns where there's a course, a clock, and other people pulling you along.

The pattern that worked, again:

- Run every day, no debate, even if it's a slow shuffle
- Mix one or two longer runs in (10ks broke up the rhythm nicely)
- Don't chase pace on the streak runs; the PB shows up on its own when the legs are used to the work

Same lesson as 2020. Daily beats hard, and consistency compounds.
